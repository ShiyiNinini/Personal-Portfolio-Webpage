Steve Jobs (1955â€“2011) was an American entrepreneur, inventor, and co-founder of Apple Inc. Known for his visionary leadership and innovative mind, Jobs revolutionized multiple industries, including personal computing, music, mobile phones, and digital publishing. Apple Inc.: Jobs co-founded Apple in 1976 alongside Steve Wozniak. They introduced the Apple I and Apple II computers, which were among the first highly successful personal computers. The Macintosh: In 1984, Jobs launched the Macintosh, the first mass-market personal computer featuring a graphical user interface and a mouse. Pixar: After being ousted from Apple in 1985, Jobs acquired a small animation company, which became Pixar Animation Studios. Under his leadership, Pixar created groundbreaking films like Toy Story, and later, he sold Pixar to Disney. Return to Apple: Jobs returned to Apple in 1997 when the company was struggling. He spearheaded a remarkable turnaround with iconic products like the iMac, iPod, iPhone, and iPad, cementing Apple's reputation for sleek design and user-friendly technology. Legacy: Jobs is remembered not only for his products but also for his relentless pursuit of perfection, attention to design, and ability to anticipate market needs.
